About

Miami Valley Symphony Orchestra

A legacy of musical excellence serving Dayton and the Miami Valley region.

Founded in 1953, the Miami Valley Symphony Orchestra emerged from a simple desire to bring world-class orchestral music to the Dayton community. What began as a small ensemble of passionate musicians has grown into a 70-member professional orchestra performing over 6 concerts annually.

MVSO's mission is rooted in three core values: Entertain audiences with the greatest works ever written for orchestra, Enrich the cultural life of our region, and Inspire the next generation of musicians and music lovers.

Today, MVSO stands as one of the region's premier cultural institutions, known for bold programming, community engagement, and a deep commitment to artistic excellence. Our musicians hail from across North America, bringing diverse perspectives and world-class training to every performance.

Our History

Seven decades of musical excellence and cultural impact in the Miami Valley.

1953
The Founding — A group of musicians and community leaders united to establish the Miami Valley Symphony Orchestra, with conductor Herbert Wellington leading the inaugural performance.
1965
Growth & Recognition — MVSO expands from 35 to 55 musicians and receives its first major grant from the Dayton Arts Foundation.
1978
New Era — Victor Martinelli becomes Music Director, introducing innovative programming that significantly increases community attendance and regional reputation.
1995
Golden Era — Margaret Chen takes the helm as Music Director. MVSO reaches its full complement of 70 musicians and expands to 8 concerts annually.
2010
National Recognition — Under George Thompson's leadership, MVSO performs at the American Orchestra League Conference and receives numerous Grammy nominations for recordings.
2018
Contemporary Vision — James Patterson becomes Music Director, emphasizing contemporary composers, community engagement, and educational initiatives.

Auditions

Join our ensemble of world-class musicians. We hold auditions twice yearly.

The Miami Valley Symphony Orchestra actively recruits talented musicians from across the region and beyond. Our open auditions are held in the spring and fall for most orchestral positions.

We are committed to building an inclusive and diverse ensemble that reflects the richness of our community.

♪

Audition Details

Repertoire Requirements

  • One passage from a major orchestral work in your primary instrument
  • A contrasting solo piece or orchestral excerpt
  • Scales in two keys (specified by the conductor)

What to Bring

  • Photo ID
  • Your sheet music (in original key)
  • Contact information
  • Résumé of musical experience
♪

Ready to audition?

Contact our administrative office to schedule your audition slot. Spring auditions are held March 15–18. Fall auditions are held September 20–23.
